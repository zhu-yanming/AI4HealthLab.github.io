Computational Histopathology

Computational histopathology enables large-scale, quantitative analysis of digital pathology for cancer diagnosis and prognosis. We develop deep learning models to extract cellular and tissue-level biomarkers from whole-slide images, revealing tumour microenvironment characteristics that are difficult to measure manually. Our work spans:

  • Biomarker Discovery and Cancer Analysis
  • Nuclei and Cell Segmentation

Computational Neuroscience

Computational neuroscience helps to advance neural science and clinical neurology by studying how brain structure and function can be computationally modelled and decoded using neuroimaging and machine learning. We develop AI-driven computational models to analyse brain signals and neuroimaging data, aiming to decode cognition, predict disorders, and map brain connectivity with high precision. Our specific research topics include:

  • EEG-based Visual Brain Decoding
  • fMRI-based Brain Disorder Diagnosis
  • White Matter Tract Segmentation from dMRI

Computational Microscopy

Computational microscopy enables automated and high-resolution analysis of cellular and subcellular processes in biological imaging. We build deep learning models to enhance, segment, and track microscopy data, supporting biological discovery and live-cell analysis at scale. Our specific research topics include:

  • Cell Segmentation and Tracking
  • Digital Fluorescence Staining

Medical Vision-Language AI

Medical visual–language AI integrates medical imaging with clinical text to support intelligent diagnostic reasoning and multimodal clinical decision-making. We develop vision-language models capable of understanding, generating, and reasoning over medical data beyond correlation-based pattern recognition. Our specific research topics include:

  • Medical Causal Reasoning
  • Medical Report Generation
  • Multimodal Learning

Trustworthy AI for Healthcare

Our research focuses on making medical AI reliable, explainable, and clinically deployable. We develop robust and explainable models that generalise across hospitals, scanners, and patient populations, supporting fair and accountable medical decision support. Our specific research topics include:

  • Explainable AI
  • Gene Mutation Prediction from CT images
  • Multimodal Medical AI (Imaging + Clinical Data)

Foundational Vision and Learning Systems

Beyond healthcare applications, we explore core computer vision and deep learning methods that enable general-purpose visual intelligence. This includes generative modelling, 3D reconstruction, adversarial sample generation, privacy preserving learning, and cross-domain adaptation. These methods form the algorithmic backbone that powers our applied research in medical and biological AI. Specific research topics include:

  • Image Enhancement and Generation
  • 3D Reconstruction and Super-Resolution
  • Adversarial Sample Generation
  • Privacy Preserving Learning
  • Unsupervised Domain Adaption
